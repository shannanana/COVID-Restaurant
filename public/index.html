Overview

This project examines the evolving situation and challenges faced by the global restaurant industry under the impact of pandemic (February 2020 - present) specifically through the lens of changes in customer behavior and public sentiment towards the pandemic.

Global View

Over the past year, the COVID-19 pandemic has ignited a socio-economic crisis like no other; a crisis that has undermined global stability and solidarity. It has stressed how interdependent health and the economy are.


With 52 million reported cases and 1.3 million deaths, has shown the dire consequences of chronic under-investment in health on the global economy and on the lives and livelihoods of billions worldwide.

Impact of COVID-19 on Social Mobility

With spread of COVID-19, most countries have issued policies or encouraged people to stay at home. This also impact restaurants, retail stores, businesses and some public facilities. We want to exam how effective have these policies been in reducing human movement? What impact has it had on how people across the world work; live; and where they visit? And how does the change in people's living style impact restaurant industry? We hereby exam the mobility data which shows how visitors to (or time spent in) categorized places change compared to our baseline days(the same day last year)
